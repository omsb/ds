Change Management and Workforce Readiness

Transforming processes and technology is only part of the challenge – the success of OMSB’s digital strategy ultimately hinges on its people. **Effective change management and workforce development** will ensure that OMSB’s staff, trainees, and stakeholders are ready, willing, and able to embrace the new digital ways of working. This strategy embeds multiple initiatives to prepare the workforce and manage the human side of change.

Digital Skills Development Program

OMSB will launch a structured program to **upskill its workforce** in digital competencies. Starting in 2026, a baseline **Digital Skills Assessment** will identify specific gaps. Based on this, OMSB will create a **Digital Skills Training Plan** (in-house workshops, online courses, certifications). The goal is to raise the percentage of staff with formal digital skills training from ~22% in 2024 to at least **75% by 2028, and 100% by 2031**. Key training will cover new system usage, general digital literacy, and advanced functional topics (e.g., data analytics for QA staff). Clinical educators will also receive orientation to champion new tools like the e-portfolio.

Change Champions and User Involvement

A network of **Change Champions** will be cultivated at the grassroots level. Staff members who are enthusiastic about digital improvements will be nominated from each area to participate early in projects (requirements, testing, training-of-trainers). This creates **local go-to people** who can assist colleagues, easing the adoption curve through peer support. Additionally, OMSB will involve end-users (staff, trainees, program directors) in the design process through **user focus groups and beta testing**. By incorporating user feedback loops, OMSB not only improves the solutions but also increases user buy-in because they feel heard and see their input valued.

Communication and Engagement

OMSB will implement a **proactive communication plan** based on the guiding principle of **“no surprises.”** Internal channels will include monthly town hall meetings, newsletters, and a dedicated intranet portal to share progress, address concerns, and celebrate successes. Externally, changes for residents, applicants, and other users will be communicated via email, SMS, social media, and the website, with early **awareness campaigns** before new services launch. Communication will be **two-way**, actively soliciting feedback and questions, possibly through a dedicated email or hotline for transformation-related queries.

Managing Resistance with Empathy

Resistance to change will be addressed with **empathy and support**. Managers will be trained in change management to listen to concerns, acknowledge difficulties, and provide encouragement. Struggling staff will receive **additional one-on-one coaching or refresher training**. Feedback channels will be created for staff to express issues anonymously, allowing the project team to adjust interfaces or provide clarifications. For external, less tech-savvy users, OMSB will offer helpdesks and tutorials (like **short how-to videos** and user manuals). By showing understanding and avoiding immediate penalization for confusion, OMSB fosters a safe environment to learn and adapt.

Incentives and Accountability

To further motivate adoption, OMSB will align certain performance incentives with digital transformation goals. Digital adoption metrics will be incorporated into **staff performance evaluations by 2029** (e.g., evaluating how well units have transitioned). **Awards or recognition** will be given to departments or individuals who innovate or achieve notable improvements. Conversely, accountability measures will require justification if a department is not utilizing an available digital service. The culture shift will be reinforced by **leadership modeling behavior** (e.g., senior leaders consistently using dashboards) to signal that the new way is the expected and rewarded way of working.

Vendor and Partner Enablement

For projects involving external parties, OMSB will institute **knowledge transfer requirements in all vendor contracts**. Vendors must conduct comprehensive training and handover sessions to OMSB’s IT staff and administrators before project closure. A **mentorship approach** will pair vendor experts with OMSB staff during implementation to ensure skills are transferred through on-the-job learning. Key external partners (like MOH IT staff for integrated services or hospital administrators) will be invited to **joint training or orientation sessions** to promote a shared understanding and smooth collaboration across organizational boundaries.

Investing in People and Culture

In essence, OMSB’s change management and workforce readiness plan is about **investing in people as much as in technology**. By training staff, involving them in the change, communicating clearly, and building a supportive culture, OMSB will turn what could be a disruptive overhaul into an inclusive journey that people are part of. This approach ensures the strategy delivers real-world benefits – because modern systems can only succeed if they are used **correctly and willingly** by a digitally empowered workforce. OMSB is aiming for the best of both: modern systems and a digitally empowered workforce.
