Strategic Pillars and Key Initiatives

To achieve the above objectives, OMSB’s digital strategy is organized into six strategic pillars. Each pillar represents a focus area of transformation and encompasses a set of initiatives or projects. These pillars ensure all dimensions of digital transformation – process, technology, data, people, and governance – are addressed in a coherent way. Below are the six pillars and their key initiatives:

1. Modernize & Automate Core Processes

Digitize and streamline every major OMSB service end-to-end.

This pillar focuses on eliminating paper-based steps and manual bottlenecks in workflows such as trainee registration, exam applications, certification issuance, program accreditation, and more. All core services will be re-engineered with a **digital-first mindset** - for example, online e-services will be developed for every major transaction (residency applications, exam scheduling, CPD logging, accreditation submissions, etc.), with unnecessary steps removed and clear service standards (turnaround times, SLAs) enforced. By redesigning processes, OMSB expects to dramatically shorten service delivery times and improve accuracy. Where appropriate, **Robotic Process Automation (RPA)** will be introduced for high-volume repetitive tasks (e.g. data entry, document verification) to further boost efficiency. A key goal is to move the majority of services to the government’s “integrative” service category – meaning fully online and, where relevant, integrated with other agencies – as opposed to merely informational or partially online services. In practical terms, this could mean a resident can apply for a program, pay fees, upload documents, and receive acceptance or scheduling entirely through an online portal without visiting OMSB; or a hospital can apply for training program accreditation and track the review status digitally. By 2030, the aim is **100% digital availability** of OMSB services, resulting in faster turnaround, higher transparency, and significant reduction in administrative workload.

2. Unify Data & Break Down Silos

Establish a single source of truth for OMSB’s data and enable seamless data sharing.

Under this pillar, OMSB will build a robust data management and integration framework. Key initiatives include creating a cloud-based **Data Lake and Enterprise Data Warehouse** that consolidate data from all OMSB systems (LMS, e-portfolio, exams, HR, accreditation, etc.). A priority project is to develop a **Master Professional Index (MPI)** – a unified master database assigning a unique ID to each individual in OMSB’s records (residents, fellows, alumni, faculty, etc.) – to track their information and transactions across all services. Significant effort will go into **cleaning and harmonizing data** (addressing the ~50% field inconsistencies found at baseline) so that information is consistent and reliable. OMSB will also form a **Data Governance Committee** to establish data standards, definitions, and access policies aligned with national guidelines (e.g. data classification levels, open data policies). On the integration front, the pillar will implement a unified integration middleware or API gateway, leveraging Oman’s **National Integration Platform (NIP)** where possible, to connect OMSB’s systems and to enable data exchange with external entities via secure APIs. For instance, once in place, this will allow a trainee’s data to be entered once in the core system and then automatically populate into the exam system, CPD system, etc., eliminating duplicate data entry. Breaking down data silos not only improves internal efficiency but also unlocks comprehensive analytics (since all data can be analyzed together) and better services (e.g. an employer verifying a graduate’s credentials can get instantaneous confirmation from the unified database rather than waiting for manual checks).

3. Embed Analytics & AI

Use data analytics and artificial intelligence to enhance decision-making and operational efficiency.

This pillar will transform OMSB into a truly **data-driven organization** by embedding real-time analytics and intelligent automation into its processes. Key initiatives include deploying **business intelligence dashboards** that provide leadership and departments with real-time insights on performance (e.g. exam pass rates, residency fill rates, service processing times) and compliance metrics. **Advanced analytics tools** will be used for trend analysis and forecasting – for example, predicting future specialty workforce needs in Oman, identifying trainees at risk of underperformance (so interventions can be made early), and analyzing program outcomes to inform curriculum improvements. OMSB will pilot and scale **AI solutions** in various areas - an AI-driven recommendation engine to suggest personalized learning or CPD opportunities to doctors based on their profile; chatbots or virtual assistants on the OMSB portal to handle common queries 24/7; and AI-based proctoring or automated grading for exams to improve efficiency and integrity. Over time, machine learning models will be developed using the rich unified data to continuously optimize operations (e.g. an algorithm to optimize exam scheduling or match residents to training posts based on multiple criteria). The aim is not just retrospective reporting (hindsight) but **predictive and prescriptive analytics** – allowing OMSB to foresee issues and opportunities and act on data-driven recommendations. Embracing analytics and AI aligns with the government’s push for “smart government” services and will directly improve outcomes like pass rates, on-time completions, and resource planning.

4. Deliver User-Centric Experiences

Place the end-user at the center of all digital services and interactions.

This pillar is about fundamentally improving how users (whether they are residents, faculty, or external stakeholders) experience OMSB’s services. OMSB will adopt a **mobile-first, omni-channel service delivery model**. That means key services will be optimized for smartphones and tablets from the start, not just desktops, and users can seamlessly switch between channels – for example, starting an application on a web portal, then completing it on a mobile app, or checking status via a self-service kiosk at OMSB if needed. A **unified OMSB Services Portal** will be developed as the one-stop shop for all e-services, integrating functionalities that are currently spread across separate systems (with single sign-on so one account accesses all). This portal will be fully **bilingual (Arabic/English)** and meet international **accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1 AA)** so that it’s usable by people with disabilities. To ensure the services truly meet user needs, OMSB will implement continuous **user feedback mechanisms** – such as satisfaction surveys, a feedback form on the portal, usage analytics, and possibly user focus groups – and use this input to drive ongoing UX improvements. Additionally, the strategy includes robust user engagement and outreach - OMSB will increase awareness of its digital services through user guides, video tutorials, webinars for stakeholders, and even periodic workshops so that all users know what digital options are available and how to use them. A formal **complaints and e-participation system** will be introduced, allowing users to log issues or suggest improvements online, aligning with the national emphasis on citizen-centric services. By focusing relentlessly on user-centric design and support, OMSB expects to significantly boost the adoption of its e-services (aiming for the majority of all transactions to happen online) and to achieve high satisfaction ratings.

5. Strengthen Governance, Security & Compliance

Establish strong digital governance and ensure all initiatives meet high standards of cybersecurity, risk management, and regulatory compliance.

This pillar addresses the “people and policies” side of transformation. OMSB will formalize its **digital governance** by setting up dedicated bodies - a Digital Transformation Steering Committee, an IT Governance Committee, and a Data Governance Committee. Clear roles and responsibilities will be defined (e.g. an executive sponsor for the transformation, project owners for major initiatives, and change champions in each department) so that accountability is in place. A comprehensive **IT Governance Framework** document will be developed by 2026. On the security front, OMSB will implement a formal **Information Security Management System (ISMS)** aligned with **ISO 27001** standards and Oman’s national cybersecurity mandates. This involves updating or creating all key security policies and procedures. Early action will be taken to close known compliance gaps – notably, adopting the National Digital Accessibility Policy and establishing an official Internet Usage Policy for staff by 2026. OMSB will maintain its alignment with the national “cloud-first” policy and will develop an Open Data Policy. Cybersecurity capabilities will be significantly bolstered - regular vulnerability assessments and penetration tests will be conducted; all users will receive cybersecurity awareness training; **multi-factor authentication (MFA)** will be enforced; and OMSB aims to set up a **Security Operations Center (SOC)** – possibly via an outsourced service – for continuous monitoring by 2027. Additionally, **100% of new applications** will undergo security testing and privacy impact assessments before launch, moving up from the current 0% compliance. OMSB commits to meeting all Government IT compliance indicators by 2026. In essence, this pillar ensures that robust management structures, security safeguards, and compliance checks are built into the transformation, thereby managing risks and sustaining the change.

6. Empower Workforce & Foster Digital Innovation

Invest in people and create a culture that embraces change and continuous innovation.

OMSB recognizes that technology alone cannot transform an organization – success also depends on the capabilities and mindset of its people. Under this pillar, a comprehensive **Digital Skills & Culture Program** will be launched. By early 2026, OMSB will conduct a digital skills assessment for all staff to identify gaps. Based on this, a targeted training and upskilling plan will roll out so that **100% of relevant administrative staff** complete certified digital skills training by 2027 (up from the current ~22%). OMSB will establish a network of **Change Champions** across departments. A “train-the-trainer” approach will empower them to coach colleagues and help troubleshoot issues, which will greatly aid user adoption and reduce resistance. Regular communication and engagement are also part of change management - OMSB will implement a communications plan with frequent updates. To foster an innovation-friendly culture, OMSB will encourage a “digital-first, innovation-always” mindset. This could include setting up a small **Digital Innovation Lab or Taskforce** that experiments with emerging technologies and incubates new ideas. OMSB may run hackathons or idea challenges. A dedicated **innovation budget** will be earmarked to pilot novel solutions. Crucially, **change management will be embedded in every project**. By empowering its workforce and nurturing innovation, OMSB ensures that the transformation is not a one-time top-down project but a sustainable, evolving journey with buy-in at all levels. Over time, this will result in an organization that continuously improves itself and can adapt to new challenges beyond 2035.

These six pillars collectively address all facets of OMSB’s digital transformation. They are interdependent and mutually reinforcing – for example, automating processes (Pillar 1) must go hand-in-hand with upskilling staff and change management (Pillar 6) so that employees can effectively operate new digital workflows. Strengthening governance and unifying data (Pillars 5 and 2) provide the solid foundation needed for advanced analytics and AI (Pillar 3). Meanwhile, an improved user experience (Pillar 4) is the ultimate outcome that all the other pillars strive to deliver. By pursuing initiatives in each pillar in parallel, OMSB will ensure that technology, processes, data, and people all advance together towards the 2035 vision.
